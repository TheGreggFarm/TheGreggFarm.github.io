Keep Spreading Lindsey love

Lindsey Stirling has touched many lives through her words and music, including mine. Lindsey's life has had it's challenges, and she's used different techniques to help overcome those challenges. I haven't seen any comprehensive list of the techniques and resources she used, so I decided to assemble what I could find.
Lindsey's faith is a core component of who she is. It gives her strength in times of need, and it would be a disservice to not mention it when talking about how she overcame her depression and anorexia.
Lindsey is a Mormon, a member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints.
The Inner Dialogue
She began to listen to her inner voice, and when she started to think negative things, would turn it around and say something positive.
Prepping for the day.
Preparing the mind for the day, the same way we prepare the body. Lindsey used Meditation and visualization.
Professional Help.
Get help from those trained to help you.
Note: Everything in the above list was from Lindsey Stirling How I broke my cycle of depression.
Gratitude Journal
The Gratitude Journal is a book you write down things you are greatful for. She discusses it in this podcast The Good Parts with Andy Grammar Podcast
Vision Board
The Vision Board is a tool used to help clarify, concentrate and maintain focus on a specific life goal. She discusses it in this Andy Grammar podcast also: The Good Parts with Andy Grammar Podcast
The King James Bible
The King James Bible is one of the Translations accepted by the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints. There are multiple free applications to access the Bible.
The Book of Mormon
The Book of Mormon is another testament of Jesus Christ. I got my copy digitally, through an app. You can however request a free copy here.
Life Without Ed by Jenni Schaefer
This book helped Lindsey cope with her Anorexia, and realize her disorder didn't have to be part of her.
